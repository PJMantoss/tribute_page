Nasir Olu Dara Jones

Creator of the world's most influential hiphop album

Nas at a concert in Atlanta on March 18, 2019.

Here is a timeline of Nasir Jones' life.

  • 1973 - Born in Brooklyn, New York, US.
  • 1988 - Met Large Professor who became his mentor and started recording his demo.
  • 1991 - Began his musical career as a featured artiste on Main Source's 'Live at the Barbecue'.
  • 1992 - Met MC Search and signed a record deal with Columbia Records
  • 1994 - Released his debut album illmatic which received universal acclaim from both critics and the hip hop community.
  • 1996 - Debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 chart with his follow up album It Was Written, which stayed on top for four consecutive weeks, went Double Platinum in two months, and made Nas internationally known..
  • 1997 - Formed The Firm(a hiphop supergroup) with Foxy Brown, AZ & Nature, signed with Dr. Dre's Aftermath, and released their debut Billboard #1 album titled 'The Firm'.
  • 1999 - Released his second best-selling record I Am which entered the Billboard 200 chart at #1
  • 2001 - Released the classic diss track Ether from the album Stillmatic, a response to Jay Z's 'take over'. New York City hip-hop radio station Hot 97 issued a poll asking listeners which rapper made the better diss song; Nas won with 58% while Jay-Z got 42% of the votes.[
  • 2002 - He released God's son which peaked at #1 on Top R&B/Hip-Hop Album charts and includes his biggest hit single ever - I Can
  • 2005 - Married R&B singer Kelis in Atlanta after a two-year relationship.
  • 2006 - Signed with Def Jam records and released the Billboard 200 #1 album Hip Hop Is Dead. Won the BET 'I Am Hip Hop Icon Award'
  • 2007 - Performed at a free concert for the Virginia Tech student body and faculty, following the school shooting there.
  • 2010 - Collaborated with Damian Marley on the studio album Distant Relatives. Its proceeds were channeled to a charity project in Africa (a school built in Congo).
  • 2013 - Invested an undisclosed six-figure sum into Mass Appeal Magazine and a technology start up company called Proven. Honored by Harvard University, as the institution established the Nasir Jones Hip-Hop Fellowship, which would serve to fund scholars and artists who show potential and creativity in the arts in connection to hip hop
  • 2014 - Partnered with job placement startup company Koru and fund a scholarship for 10 college graduates.
  • 2015 - Co-founded a soul food restaurant Sweet Chick with branches in New York, Los Angeles and London

"Nas is perhaps contemporary rap's greatest innovator in storytelling. His catalog includes songs narrated before birth ('Fetus') and after death ('Amongst Kings'), biographies ('UBR [Unauthorized Biography of Rakim]') and autobiographies ('Doo Rags'), allegorical tales ('Money Is My Bitch') and epistolary ones ('One Love'), he's rapped in the voice of a woman ('Sekou Story') and even of a gun ('I Gave You Power')."

--writer Adam Bradley.
